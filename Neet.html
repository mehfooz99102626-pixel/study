1. What is meant by Living World?
Living world includes all organisms that show life processes such as metabolism, growth, reproduction and response to stimuli.
2. What is biodiversity?
Biodiversity refers to the variety and variability of living organisms found on Earth.
3. Why is classification necessary?
Classification helps in organizing organisms into groups for easy study, identification and understanding of relationships.
4. What is taxonomy?
Taxonomy is the branch of biology that deals with identification, nomenclature and classification of organisms.
5. What is nomenclature?
Nomenclature is the system of assigning scientific names to organisms.
6. Who proposed binomial nomenclature?
Binomial nomenclature was proposed by Carolus Linnaeus.
7. What are taxonomic aids?
Taxonomic aids are tools like herbarium, botanical gardens, museums and keys used for identification of organisms.
8. What is a herbarium?
A herbarium is a collection of preserved plant specimens arranged systematically.
9. What are taxonomic keys?
Taxonomic keys are tools used to identify organisms based on contrasting characteristics.
10. What is a couplet?
A couplet consists of two contrasting statements used in taxonomic keys.
11. What is flora?
Flora is a book containing information about plants of a particular region.
12. What is fauna?
Fauna refers to the animal life of a particular region.
13. What is a museum?
A museum preserves plant and animal specimens for study and reference.
14. What is a zoological park?
Zoological parks conserve wild animals in protected environments.
15. What is classification?
Classification is the systematic arrangement of organisms into groups based on similarities.
16. What is systematics?
Systematics is the study of diversity of organisms and their evolutionary relationships.
17. What is species?
Species is a group of organisms that can interbreed and produce fertile offspring.
18. What is genus?
Genus is a group of related species sharing common characteristics.
19. Why are scientific names written in italics?
Scientific names are written in italics to follow international rules of nomenclature.
20. What is the importance of the Living World?
Studying the living world helps us understand life forms, evolution and biodiversity conservation.
Define biodiversity
Biodiversity is the variety of living organisms on Earth.
